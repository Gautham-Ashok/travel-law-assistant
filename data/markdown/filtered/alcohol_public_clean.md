# Alcohol & Public Drinking Laws in India — Traveller Guide

Alcohol laws in India are **not uniform**. They vary by State because alcohol is regulated under the **State List** of the Constitution of India. What is legal in one State may be an offence in another.

---

## Constitutional Basis

- Alcohol is regulated by **State Governments** under Entry 8 of the State List (Seventh Schedule).
- States decide:
  - legal drinking age,
  - sale and licensing,
  - public consumption rules,
  - prohibition (dry states).

### Role of Governments
- **Central Government**:
  - drunk driving laws (Motor Vehicles Act),
  - inter-state transport issues.
- **State Governments**:
  - excise rules,
  - public drinking penalties,
  - alcohol prohibition.

---

## Legal Drinking Age (Varies by State)

Examples:
- **18 years**: Goa, Sikkim
- **21–23 years**: Many States
- **25 years**: Delhi
- **Completely prohibited (Dry States)**:
  - Bihar
  - Gujarat
  - Mizoram
  - Nagaland
  - Lakshadweep (UT)

> Drinking or possessing alcohol in dry states is a criminal offence.

---

## Dry States (Alcohol Prohibition)

In dry states:
- Sale, possession, transport, and consumption of alcohol are illegal.

Possible consequences (varies by State):
- imprisonment (may range from months to years),
- monetary fines,
- confiscation of alcohol and vehicles.

---

## Public Drinking Laws in India

Drinking alcohol in **public places** is an offence in most States.

### Public places include:
- roads and footpaths,
- public parks and beaches,
- parking areas,
- vehicles parked in public places.

### Common violations:
- drinking inside a parked car,
- drinking on beaches or streets,
- intoxication causing public nuisance.

### Penalties (varies by State):
- fine (commonly ₹200 to ₹5,000),
- short-term imprisonment for repeat or aggravated offences,
- seizure of alcohol.

---

## Drunken Misconduct (Indian Penal Code)

- **IPC Section 510**:  
  Appearing drunk in a public place and causing annoyance to others is punishable with:
  - imprisonment up to **24 hours**, or
  - fine, or
  - both.

---

## Drunk Driving (Motor Vehicles Act)

- Driving under the influence of alcohol is a criminal offence.

Possible penalties:
- fine,
- imprisonment,
- suspension or cancellation of driving licence,
- vehicle seizure.

> Even sitting inside a vehicle while intoxicated may attract legal action in some States.

---

## Transporting Alcohol Between States

- Carrying alcohol across State borders may violate excise laws.
- Quantity limits and permissions vary by State.
- Alcohol legally purchased in one State may be illegal in another.

---

## Key Takeaways for Travellers

- Always check **State-specific alcohol laws** before drinking.
- Avoid drinking in **public places**.
- Never drink and drive.
- Alcohol is completely illegal in **dry states**.
- Penalties and enforcement vary widely across India.

---
