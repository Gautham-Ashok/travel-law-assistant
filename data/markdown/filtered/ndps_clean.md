# NDPS Act, 1985 — Key Definitions (Traveller-Relevant)

## “Addict”
A person who has dependence on any narcotic drug or psychotropic substance.

---

## “Cannabis (hemp)”
Cannabis (hemp) means:
- **Charas** — the separated resin obtained from the cannabis plant, including hashish oil or liquid hashish;
- **Ganja** — the flowering or fruiting tops of the cannabis plant (excluding seeds and leaves when not accompanied by the tops);
- Any mixture, with or without neutral material, of the above forms of cannabis or any drink prepared therefrom.

---

## “Cannabis plant”
Any plant of the genus *Cannabis*.

---

## “Charas”
The separated resin, in whatever form, obtained from the cannabis plant, including hashish oil or liquid hashish.

---

## “Ganja”
The flowering or fruiting tops of the cannabis plant, excluding seeds and leaves when not accompanied by the tops.

---

## “Narcotic drug”
Coca leaf, cannabis (hemp), opium, poppy straw and includes all manufactured drugs.

---

## “Psychotropic substance”
Any substance, natural or synthetic, or any preparation included in the list of psychotropic substances specified in the Schedule.

---

## “Small quantity”
Any quantity lesser than the quantity specified by the Central Government by notification in the Official Gazette.

---

## “Commercial quantity”
Any quantity greater than the quantity specified by the Central Government by notification in the Official Gazette.

---

## “Use”
Use, in relation to narcotic drugs and psychotropic substances, means any kind of use except personal consumption.

# NDPS Act, 1985 — Prohibition & Consumption

## Section 8: Prohibition of certain operations
No person shall—

- cultivate any coca plant or gather any portion of a coca plant; or
- cultivate the opium poppy or any cannabis plant; or
- produce, manufacture, possess, sell, purchase, transport, warehouse, use, consume,
  import inter-State, export inter-State, import into India, export from India or tranship
  any narcotic drug or psychotropic substance,

**except** for medical or scientific purposes and in the manner and to the extent provided
by the provisions of this Act or the rules or orders made thereunder, and where required,
in accordance with the terms and conditions of a valid licence, permit or authorisation.

---

## Section 27: Punishment for consumption of narcotic drugs or psychotropic substances

Whoever consumes any narcotic drug or psychotropic substance shall be punishable—

- **(a)** where the substance consumed is cocaine, morphine, diacetyl-morphine (heroin)
  or any other substance notified by the Central Government,
  with rigorous imprisonment for a term which may extend to **one year**, or with
  fine which may extend to **₹20,000**, or with both; or

- **(b)** where the substance consumed is other than those specified above,
  with imprisonment for a term which may extend to **six months**, or with
  fine which may extend to **₹10,000**, or with both.


# NDPS Act, 1985 — Possession, Quantity & Punishment

## Section 20: Punishment for contravention in relation to cannabis plant and cannabis

Whoever, in contravention of any provision of this Act or any rule, order or condition of licence granted thereunder—

- **(a)** cultivates any cannabis plant; or  
- **(b)** produces, manufactures, possesses, sells, purchases, transports, imports inter-State,
  exports inter-State or uses cannabis,

shall be punishable—

- **(i)** where the offence relates to cultivation of cannabis plant,  
  with rigorous imprisonment which may extend to **10 years**, and with fine which may extend to **₹1,00,000**;

- **(ii)** where the offence relates to cannabis (other than cultivation):
  - **(A) Small quantity** — rigorous imprisonment up to **1 year**, or fine up to **₹10,000**, or both;
  - **(B) Quantity less than commercial but greater than small** — rigorous imprisonment up to **10 years** and fine up to **₹1,00,000**;
  - **(C) Commercial quantity** — rigorous imprisonment **not less than 10 years and up to 20 years**, and fine **₹1,00,000 to ₹2,00,000**  
    (court may impose higher fine for recorded reasons).

---

## Section 21: Punishment for contravention in relation to manufactured drugs and preparations

Whoever manufactures, possesses, sells, purchases, transports, imports inter-State,
exports inter-State or uses any manufactured drug or any preparation containing any manufactured drug shall be punishable—

- **(a) Small quantity** — rigorous imprisonment up to **1 year**, or fine up to **₹10,000**, or both;
- **(b) Quantity less than commercial but greater than small** — rigorous imprisonment up to **10 years** and fine up to **₹1,00,000**;
- **(c) Commercial quantity** — rigorous imprisonment **not less than 10 years and up to 20 years**, and fine **₹1,00,000 to ₹2,00,000**  
  (court may impose higher fine for recorded reasons).

---

## Section 22: Punishment for contravention in relation to psychotropic substances

Whoever manufactures, possesses, sells, purchases, transports, imports inter-State,
exports inter-State or uses any psychotropic substance shall be punishable—

- **(a) Small quantity** — rigorous imprisonment up to **1 year**, or fine up to **₹10,000**, or both;
- **(b) Quantity less than commercial but greater than small** — rigorous imprisonment up to **10 years** and fine up to **₹1,00,000**;
- **(c) Commercial quantity** — rigorous imprisonment **not less than 10 years and up to 20 years**, and fine **₹1,00,000 to ₹2,00,000**  
  (court may impose higher fine for recorded reasons).

---

## Section 23: Punishment for illegal import into India, export from India or transhipment

Whoever illegally imports into India, exports from India or tranships any narcotic drug
or psychotropic substance shall be punishable—

- **(a) Small quantity** — rigorous imprisonment up to **1 year**, or fine up to **₹10,000**, or both;
- **(b) Quantity less than commercial but greater than small** — rigorous imprisonment up to **10 years** and fine up to **₹1,00,000**;
- **(c) Commercial quantity** — rigorous imprisonment **not less than 10 years and up to 20 years**, and fine **₹1,00,000 to ₹2,00,000**  
  (court may impose higher fine for recorded reasons).

# NDPS Act, 1985 — Bail & Presumptions

## Section 37: Offences to be cognizable and non-bailable

Notwithstanding anything contained in the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973—

- **(a)** every offence punishable under this Act shall be **cognizable**; and
- **(b)** no person accused of an offence under **section 19, section 24, section 27A, or any offence involving commercial quantity** shall be released on bail or on his own bond unless—
  - **(i)** the Public Prosecutor has been given an opportunity to oppose the application for such release; and
  - **(ii)** where the Public Prosecutor opposes the application, the court is satisfied that there are reasonable grounds for believing that the accused is **not guilty** of such offence and that he is **not likely to commit any offence while on bail**.

The limitations on granting bail under this section are **in addition to** the limitations under the Code of Criminal Procedure or any other law in force.

---

## Section 35: Presumption of culpable mental state

In any prosecution for an offence under this Act which requires a culpable mental state, the court shall **presume the existence of such mental state**.

It shall be a defence for the accused to prove that he had **no such mental state** with respect to the act charged.

**Explanation:**  
“Culpable mental state” includes intention, motive, knowledge of a fact, belief in, or reason to believe, a fact.

For the purpose of this section, a fact is said to be proved only when the court believes it to exist **beyond a reasonable doubt**, and not merely by a preponderance of probability.

---

## Section 54: Presumption from possession of illicit articles

In trials under this Act, it may be presumed, unless the contrary is proved, that the accused has committed an offence in respect of—

- any narcotic drug, psychotropic substance or controlled substance;
- any opium poppy, cannabis plant or coca plant growing on land cultivated by him;
- any apparatus or utensils specially designed or adapted for the manufacture of narcotic drugs or psychotropic substances;
- any materials or residues used in or resulting from the manufacture of narcotic drugs or psychotropic substances,

**if the accused fails to account satisfactorily for such possession.**
