This section explains what the law provides regarding arrest, detention, rights, and remedies
under the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (CrPC).

---

## üö® 1. Arrest Without Warrant (Section 41, CrPC)

A police officer may arrest a person without a warrant under the following circumstances:

- If the person commits a **cognizable offence** in the presence of a police officer;
- If there exists a **reasonable complaint, credible information, or reasonable suspicion**
  that the person has committed a cognizable offence punishable with imprisonment
  which may extend to **seven years or more**;
- If arrest is necessary to:
  - prevent further offences,
  - ensure proper investigation,
  - prevent tampering with evidence,
  - prevent intimidation of witnesses,
  - ensure the presence of the accused before court;
- If the person is found in possession of property reasonably suspected to be stolen;
- If the person obstructs a police officer in the execution of duty or attempts to escape
  lawful custody;
- If the person has committed an offence outside India which is punishable under Indian law
  and is liable for arrest under extradition laws.

## üìã 2. Procedure and Rights During Arrest  
(Sections 41B, 50, 50A, CrPC)

At the time of arrest, the following safeguards are mandatory:

- **Identification of Officer:**  
  The police officer making the arrest must bear a clear and visible name and identification.

- **Arrest Memo:**  
  A Memorandum of Arrest must be prepared:
  - signed by at least one witness (family member or respectable person), and
  - countersigned by the arrested person.

- **Right to Inform:**  
  The arrested person has the right to inform a friend, relative, or any nominated person
  about the arrest and place of detention.

- **Grounds of Arrest:**  
  The arrested person must be informed **immediately** of the grounds of arrest.

- **Information Regarding Bail:**  
  If the offence is bailable, the person must be informed of the right to be released on bail.

## ‚öñÔ∏è 3. Detention and Legal Representation  
(Sections 41D, 57, CrPC)

- **Right to a Lawyer:**  
  The arrested person is entitled to meet and consult an advocate of their choice during
  interrogation (though not throughout interrogation).

- **24-Hour Rule:**  
  No person may be detained in police custody for more than **24 hours** without being
  produced before a Magistrate, excluding travel time.

---

## ü©∫ 4. Health, Safety, and Search Procedures  
(Sections 51, 54, 55A, CrPC)

- **Medical Examination:**  
  Every arrested person must be medically examined by a registered medical practitioner.
  A copy of the medical report must be provided to the arrested person or their nominee.

- **Responsibility for Safety:**  
  The authority having custody of the arrested person is legally responsible for their
  health and safety.

- **Search and Seizure:**  
  If a search is conducted, the arrested person is entitled to receive a receipt for
  all seized articles (excluding necessary wearing apparel).

- **Use of Restraint:**  
  Restraints shall not be used unless strictly necessary to prevent escape.

---

## üë© Special Protections for Women  
(Sections 46, 51, 54, CrPC)

- **Time of Arrest:**  
  No woman shall be arrested **between sunset and sunrise**, except in exceptional
  circumstances with prior written permission of a Judicial Magistrate.

- **Female Officers:**  
  Arrest and body search of a woman must be carried out only by a **female police officer**.

- **Privacy and Decency:**  
  Searches and medical examinations must be conducted with strict regard to dignity,
  privacy, and decency.

## üÜò Remedies and Compensation

### üí∞ Victim Compensation Scheme (Section 357A, CrPC)

- State Governments shall provide funds for compensation to victims or their dependents
  who have suffered loss or injury due to crime.
- Compensation may be granted even if the offender is **not identified or traced**.
- Victims are entitled to **immediate free medical treatment** at hospitals upon certification
  by a police officer or Magistrate.

---

### ‚ùå Compensation for Groundless Arrest (Section 358, CrPC)

- If a person causes another to be arrested **without sufficient grounds**, a Magistrate
  may order compensation for:
  - loss of time, and
  - expenses incurred by the arrested person.
