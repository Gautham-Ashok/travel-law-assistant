# Noise Pollution (Regulation and Control) Rules, 2000 — Key Provisions

---

## Section 2: Definitions (Selected)

### “Public place”
Any place to which the public have access, whether as of right or not, and includes auditoriums, hotels, public waiting rooms, convention centres, public offices, shopping malls, cinema halls, educational institutions, libraries, open grounds and the like which are visited by the general public.

### “Night time”
The period between **10.00 p.m. and 6.00 a.m.**

### “Area / Zone”
All areas which fall in either of the four categories given in the Schedule annexed to these rules.

### “Authority”
Any authority or officer authorised by the Central Government or the State Government in accordance with law, and includes a District Magistrate, Police Commissioner, or any officer not below the rank of Deputy Superintendent of Police designated for maintenance of ambient air quality standards in respect of noise.

---

## Section 3: Ambient Air Quality Standards in Respect of Noise

1. Ambient air quality standards in respect of noise for different areas/zones shall be as specified in the Schedule.
2. The State Government shall categorise areas into industrial, commercial, residential or silence areas/zones for implementation of noise standards.
5. An area comprising **not less than 100 metres** around hospitals, educational institutions and courts may be declared as a **silence zone** by the State Government.

> An area shall not fall under the silence zone category unless notified by the State Government.

---

## Section 4: Responsibility for Enforcement

1. Noise levels in any area/zone shall not exceed the ambient air quality standards specified in the Schedule.
2. The authority shall be responsible for enforcement of noise pollution control measures and compliance with noise standards.

---

## Section 5: Restrictions on Loudspeakers and Public Address Systems

1. Loudspeakers or public address systems shall not be used without **written permission** from the authority.
2. Loudspeakers, public address systems, sound amplifiers or musical instruments shall **not be used during night time**, except:
   - in closed premises such as auditoriums, conference rooms or community halls, or
   - during a public emergency.
3. State Governments may permit use of loudspeakers during night hours **between 10.00 p.m. and 12.00 midnight** for cultural, religious or festive occasions, subject to conditions, for a maximum of **15 days in a calendar year**.
4. Noise level at the boundary of a public place using loudspeakers shall not exceed **10 dB(A)** above the ambient standard or **75 dB(A)**, whichever is lower.
5. Noise from private sound systems shall not exceed **5 dB(A)** above the ambient noise standard at the boundary of the private place.

---

## Section 5A: Restrictions on Horns, Construction Equipment and Firecrackers

1. No horn shall be used in silence zones or during night time in residential areas, except in a public emergency.
2. Sound-emitting firecrackers shall not be burst in silence zones or during night time.
3. Sound-emitting construction equipment shall not be used during night time in residential areas and silence zones.

---

## Section 6: Consequences of Violation in Silence Zone

Any person committing the following acts in a silence zone shall be liable for penalty under law:

- Playing music or using sound amplifiers;
- Beating drums, blowing horns or sounding musical or pressure instruments;
- Exhibiting musical, mimetic or other performances attracting crowds;
- Bursting sound-emitting firecrackers;
- Using loudspeakers or public address systems.

---

## Section 7: Complaints

1. Any person may make a complaint to the authority if:
   - noise exceeds ambient standards by **10 dB(A)** or more; or
   - restrictions during night time are violated.
2. The authority shall act on the complaint and take action against the violator as per law.

---

## Section 8: Power to Prohibit Continuance of Noise

1. If the authority is satisfied that noise causes or is likely to cause annoyance, disturbance, discomfort or injury, it may issue **written orders** to prevent, prohibit, control or regulate:
   - vocal or instrumental music;
   - sounds from loudspeakers, horns, construction equipment or appliances;
   - bursting of sound-emitting firecrackers;
   - any trade, activity or process causing noise.
2. The authority may rescind, modify or alter such orders after giving affected parties an opportunity to be heard and recording reasons.

---

## Schedule: Ambient Noise Limits

| Area / Zone        | Day Time (6 a.m. – 10 p.m.) | Night Time (10 p.m. – 6 a.m.) |
|--------------------|----------------------------|-------------------------------|
| Industrial Area    | 75 dB(A)                   | 70 dB(A)                      |
| Commercial Area    | 65 dB(A)                   | 55 dB(A)                      |
| Residential Area   | 55 dB(A)                   | 45 dB(A)                      |
| Silence Zone       | 50 dB(A)                   | 40 dB(A)                      |
