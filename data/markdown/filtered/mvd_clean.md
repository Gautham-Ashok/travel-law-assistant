## ü™™ Driving Licence & Eligibility

### Section 3 ‚Äî Necessity for Driving Licence
No person shall drive a motor vehicle in any public place unless he holds a valid and
effective driving licence authorising him to drive that class of vehicle.

Driving a transport vehicle is permitted only if the licence specifically authorises it.

Learners may drive only under prescribed conditions.

---

### Section 4 ‚Äî Age Limit for Driving
- Minimum age **18 years** for motor vehicles
- Minimum age **16 years** for motorcycles up to **50cc**
- Minimum age **20 years** for transport vehicles

No learner‚Äôs or driving licence shall be issued unless the applicant is eligible under
this section.

## üìÑ Mandatory Documents

### Section 130 ‚Äî Duty to Produce Documents
The driver of a motor vehicle shall, on demand by a police officer in uniform, produce:
- Driving licence
- Certificate of registration

If seized earlier, a valid receipt may be produced and the document shown later as prescribed.

The owner or person in charge shall also produce on demand:
- Certificate of insurance
- Fitness certificate (transport vehicles)
- Permit (transport vehicles)

---

### Section 146 ‚Äî Necessity for Insurance
No motor vehicle shall be used in a public place unless a valid third-party insurance
policy is in force.

Vehicles carrying hazardous goods must also carry public liability insurance.

## üö¶ Traffic Rules & Road Safety

### Section 112 ‚Äî Speed Limits
No vehicle shall exceed or fall below speed limits fixed by law or traffic signage.

State Governments may impose local speed restrictions for public safety.

---

### Section 119 ‚Äî Obedience to Traffic Signs
Every driver shall obey:
- Mandatory traffic signs
- Traffic signals
- Directions of police officers regulating traffic

---

### Section 122 ‚Äî Leaving Vehicle in Dangerous Position
No vehicle shall be left on a public place in a position or condition that causes:
- Danger
- Obstruction
- Undue inconvenience to others

## üç∫ Breath and Laboratory Tests

### Section 203 ‚Äî Breath Test
Police may conduct breath tests if drunk driving is suspected.

Refusal permits arrest without warrant.

Breath test results are admissible in evidence.

---

### Section 204 ‚Äî Laboratory Test
Blood samples may be taken if:
- Breath test indicates alcohol, or
- Breath test is refused

Female persons shall be examined in the presence of a female.

Laboratory reports are admissible in evidence.

## üöë Accidents & Legal Duties

### Section 132 ‚Äî Duty to Stop
Drivers must stop when required by:
- Police officers
- Persons affected by accidents
- Animal handlers if safety is threatened

---

### Section 134 ‚Äî Duty in Case of Accident
Drivers must:
- Provide immediate medical assistance
- Report the accident to police within **24 hours**
- Inform the insurer with accident details

Emergency medical treatment shall not be delayed.

---

### Section 134A ‚Äî Protection of Good Samaritans
Persons assisting accident victims in good faith:
- Shall not be harassed
- Are protected from civil and criminal liability

---

## üëÆ Police Powers

### Section 202 ‚Äî Arrest Without Warrant
Police may arrest without warrant for:
- Dangerous driving (Section 184)
- Drunk driving (Section 185)

Drunk-driving arrests must be followed by medical examination within **2 hours**.

---

### Section 206 ‚Äî Power to Impound Documents
Police may seize:
- Driving licence
- Registration certificate
- Insurance documents

Temporary acknowledgment may be issued.

---

### Section 207 ‚Äî Power to Detain Vehicles
Vehicles may be detained for:
- No driving licence
- Under-age driving
- No registration or permit

Vehicles may be released after document verification.

---

## üí∞ Section 200 ‚Äî Compounding of Offences
Certain traffic offences may be compounded by paying a prescribed fine.

Compounding does not erase prior offence history or licence consequences.
